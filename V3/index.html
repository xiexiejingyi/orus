Core Values — O.R.U.S.

The name is the manifesto. Read down the letters and you get the four things ORUS exists to do: Open · Relate · Unplug · Stay. Every decision — the dark room, the single beam of light, the stone coasters, the wet-market pricing — serves one of these.

Open

No velvet rope, no posturing, no algorithm deciding who belongs. The door is open to the CBD suit and the weekend wanderer alike, and so is the room — honest, unfiltered, a little raw. "Open" is also a release valve: we're the place you go offline, drop the performance, and finally exhale.

Open door · open people · phones down
Relate

The whole point is the person across the coaster. ORUS is engineered for real connection — the accidental conversation, the flirty "OR… / …US" wingman line, the table that grows by one. In a city talking through screens, we make it easy to relate face to face, unknown to known.

Why choose OR · when you can choose US
Unplug

Here's the joke, and we're committing to it: the back wall is a grid of power sockets — and the one thing you switch off is you. Flick the group chat to OFF, dock the phone in the wall, and the only notifications left are a fresh round landing and someone new pulling up a stool. The most radical move these days is being fully, unapologetically here.

Flip yourself to OFF · fully here
Stay

"Go home early, or stay out late?" Down here the answer is always stay. One more pour, one more song, one more story — the cave is built to keep you a little longer than you planned. Stay close, stay late, stay part of US.

One more pour · stay a while

Color Palette

A near-black cave interior, grounded in raw ore and concrete. What we own isn't a colour, it's a pairing in the dark: one molten-ore ember breaking through, answered by a cool vein of oxidised copper. The darkness makes the room intimate; the warm ember makes it romantic; the verdigris keeps it mineral and un-generic — not the soft "sleep-amber" glow every wellness brand reaches for.

Primary
Cave Black
#0E0E0D
A deep, matte near-black — the dark, intimate interior of the cave. It signals depth, mystery and permanence, pulling guests away from the bright, plastic surface of the city and down into something grounded.
Secondary
Raw Stone
#9C958A
A warm, dusty grey — raw rock and stone coasters. Tactile and honest, it humanises the black and reinforces the "raw nature" half of the brand without feeling cold or corporate.
Neutral
Bone
#EDE7DB
A soft, warm off-white for type and breathing room. Honest rather than clinical, it carries the widely-spaced wordmark cleanly against the dark and keeps the "high design" half polished.
Accent · Ember
Molten Ore
#CF3E17
The light piercing the darkness — molten rock at the cave mouth. Deliberately deeper and redder than the trendy terracotta / "sleep-amber" orange every bar and wellness brand now uses, so it reads as lava and earned metal, not a soft glow. Kept to a single beam, never a flood — what we own is the contrast, always tied to the rough-hewn 'O'.
Accent · Earth
Oxide Vein
#7A3B2E
An oxidised, mineral red-brown — the precious vein running through the ore. It grounds the ember, adds maturity and ruggedness, and ties the identity back to earth and hidden treasure.
Signature · Cool
Verdigris
#4C8C7A
Oxidised copper — the cool patina metal earns with age. This is the differentiator: near-black + ember is warm and expected, but pairing it with a mineral teal-green is a combination a soft, bright wellness brand structurally can't follow. Used sparingly — a rim of light, a line, a stamp — it makes the palette unmistakably ours.
Flash · Signal
Sulphur
#F1C40E
Raw mineral yellow — the sulphur streak in the ore, and the brand's loud after-dark voice. This is the only high-key colour in the system, held off the interior and reserved for event posters, social and promo: blacked-out type on a full sulphur field, cut with the molten ember (see Section 06). It gives the nightlife side of ORUS a jolt of energy the calm cave never shows — the flash of the flyer, not the light of the room.

Usage ratio (interior & core): ~70% Cave Black · ~15% Raw Stone & Bone · ~8% Oxide Vein · ~4% Molten Ore · ~3% Verdigris. The ember is a single beam and verdigris a thin cool vein — never flood the room with either. The signature is the warm-vs-cool tension in the dark, not any one hue. Sulphur sits outside this ratio — it is a promo-only flash (posters, social, event graphics), never used on the space, menu or core identity.

Raw, Done Right — Rugged Can Read Premium

A quick reality check before we sand ORUS smooth. Ultra-minimal, geometric marks can end up feeling generic and a little cold — less lifestyle. The most characterful hospitality brands prove the opposite: a hand-made, rugged mark, executed with considered materials, warmth and light, reads expensive, human and unmistakably its own. This is the argument for keeping ORUS's raw, hand-hewn wordmark as the hero rather than over-refining it.

Takeaway — the goal isn't clean vs. raw — it's raw, done with intent. Keep the texture; control the materials, spacing and light around it. That's where rugged becomes premium.

Collateral & Touchpoints

Where the brand meets the space. Every physical touchpoint should feel tactile and deliberate — raw surfaces, deep shadow, one warm accent. Cheap on price, never cheap to the touch. Collateral designs are in progress; the direction below sets the tone.

Facade — Open Frontage
One move, biggest impact. An openable frontage — bi-fold panels or a lift-up awning — that folds away so the room breathes onto the five-foot-way. By day it reads as a bright, welcoming café: counter stools, greenery, spillout seating, kopitiam-open — the clearest possible signal of no velvet rope and the all-day trade. As night falls the same opening becomes the glowing cave mouth, warm ember light spilling onto the pavement and pulling people in. Keep the materials dark, raw and ownable and let openness + warm spill light do all the inviting — never generic white. The references show the range: bi-fold metal-and-glass, lift-up awning windows, and street-side spillout. (SG notes: five-foot-way spillout needs an outdoor-refreshment-area licence; keep an air-curtain / vestibule if running aircon; URA conservation facades cap signage size.)

Projected Light & Visuals
A secondary, re-skinnable layer that wraps around the hero back bar: a single projector throwing light, film grain and texture across the stone — the cave mouth becomes whatever the night calls for. This keeps the space flexible: seasonal artwork, archival film, slow abstract loops, or a fully custom takeover. It is also the more sustainable choice — no reprints, no waste — and lets the venue be re-skinned instantly for private event buyouts.

The Memory Wall — the Open Name, on display
Where the Open Name becomes a place. Guests' filled-in "What's your ORUS?" coasters and cards get clipped up alongside polaroids, receipts, ticket stubs and quick snaps — a growing, guest-built archive of what ORUS meant that night.

Free UGC, deeply community-first. Build on the kitchen window / pass (where customers usually stand while queuing for the toilet) — run it on a stainless steel magnetic board or mesh grid + clips so it flips straight into a merch wall for drops — tees, caps and totes on the very same hooks.

The Status Coaster — Read the Room
A social cue hidden inside our own mark. The seal comes in four colours, and each one signals your vibe for the night. Every table gets the full set in a small stand with a legend card; after you order, you pick the coaster that matches your mood and the server slides it under your glass as they serve — no app, no awkward opener, just a colour. Swap it the moment the night changes. Staff read the whole room at a glance, and singles or groups get a low-stakes way to say come over — or not tonight. It turns the ORUS mark itself into the icebreaker.

How to read it

Same v1.0 list, two faces. The Market Receipt (bone, mono, dashed rules) prints the flat $9.90 value plainly — honest, loud, relatable. The Ore Card is the clean, editorial twin — inspired by the mono-type reference below: a bold MENU masthead, thin-ruled list, the raw ore-rock mark and the hand-hewn ORUS wordmark signing it off. Its rules are drawn in verdigris — the palette's cool signature quietly threading the page. One shouts value, one whispers craft.

Dressing the list — the v1.0 layouts above are the working menus. The material references and story skins below are optional wrappers the same list can wear (a payslip, a market receipt, a nutrition panel) once the final direction is chosen.

Format & Material References
CONCEPT 01
The Payslip

The drink list written in the language of office life — the drinks and categories are named after office jargon (e.g. a "KPI", an "AAR", the "Off-in-Lieu"), framed as a salary statement.

Speaks straight to the after-work crowd escaping the grind, with the punchline "NET — you keep the change."

Drink names / categories Ideas:
KPI · Off-in-Lieu · AAR · Monthly Review · OT Claim
CONCEPT 02
The Market Receipt

The drinks are built on chilled juices, fruit purées, tomato and matcha — real produce — so the menu reads like a fresh-market receipt.

The drinks and categories are named like just-picked goods (a "Fresh Today", the "Cold-Pressed" section), with bold prices shouting value the way a wet-market stall does. Relatable, honest, Singaporean.

Drink names / categories Ideas:
Fresh Today · Cold-Pressed · By the Kilo · Tomato Foam · Best Before Last Call
CONCEPT 03
The Nutrition Panel

Each drink gets its own mock nutrition / ingredients label, riffing on the cold juices, purée highballs, matcha and tomato foam.

The drinks and categories are named after label language (a "100% Fresh Purée", the "% Daily Fun" section), with clean carton typography, servings and a big calorie-style price. Tongue-in-cheek wellness — looks healthy, drinks like a party.

Drink names / categories:
100% Fresh Purée · Matcha · Serving: 1 · Tomato Foam · % Daily Fun
