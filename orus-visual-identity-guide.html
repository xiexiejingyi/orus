The Idea Behind the Mark

Every design decision below traces back to the meaning of the name. ORUS isn't a dictionary word — that mystique is the point.
It lives at the intersection of raw nature and high design, and the identity has to do the same.

Ore

Raw, unrefined rock from the deepest veins of the earth — holding precious minerals. Raw potential and hidden treasure. The palette and textures are mined, not manufactured.

Light in Darkness

The moment light pierces the dark — the mouth of a cave at dawn. This is why a single molten glow always cuts through the near-black.

The Circle "O"

生命之圆 — the Circle of Life: wholeness, completeness, the eternal flowing rhythm. The "O" is treated as a perfect, courageous circle.

OR / US

Read it again: OR / US. The flirty, social wordplay that resolves the weekend's "this or that" — descend into the cave and just be part of us.

01

Color Palette

A near-black cave interior, grounded in raw ore and concrete, with one molten beam of dawn light breaking through. The darkness makes the room intimate; the single warm light makes it romantic. Tap any hex to copy.

Primary
Cave Black
#0E0E0D
A deep, matte near-black — the dark, intimate interior of the cave. It signals depth, mystery and permanence, pulling guests away from the bright, plastic surface of the city and down into something grounded.
Secondary
Raw Stone
#9C958A
A warm, dusty grey — raw rock and stone coasters. Tactile and honest, it humanises the black and reinforces the "raw nature" half of the brand without feeling cold or corporate.
Accent
Dawn Molten
#E5531A
The light piercing the darkness — the cave mouth at dawn. Warm and magnetic, it triggers appetite, intimacy and anticipation. Used as a single beam, it becomes the brand's most ownable signal.
Accent · Earth
Oxide Vein
#7A3B2E
An oxidised, mineral red-brown — the precious vein running through the ore. It grounds the bright accent, adds maturity and ruggedness, and ties the identity back to earth and hidden treasure.
Neutral
Bone
#EDE7DB
A soft, warm off-white for type and breathing room. Honest rather than clinical, it carries the widely-spaced wordmark cleanly against the dark and keeps the "high design" half polished.

Usage ratio: ~72% Cave Black · ~16% Raw Stone & Bone · ~8% Oxide Vein · ~4% Dawn Molten. The molten light is a single beam — never flood the room with it.

Typography System

The concept deck is explicit: ORUS should read as one bespoke, geometric sans-serif with letters spaced widely apart to evoke vast space and breathing room. Manrope delivers exactly that — a clean, modern geometric sans — so it carries the entire system. Hierarchy comes from weight and tracking, not from switching fonts. Free on Google Fonts.

Display · WordmarkManrope — 800 ExtraBold
O R U S
Use for: The logo, hero statements, poster headlines.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE. For the wordmark, very wide tracking (+0.5em) for "vast space." For headlines, +2–4%. Tight leading (1.0). Always add left padding equal to the tracking so it stays optically centered.

03

Logo Design Concepts

Four directions drawn straight from the deck: the widely-spaced wordmark, the OR / US wordplay, the circle "O," and a refined ink-seal icon (expanded from your reference). All built in Manrope so the system stays bespoke and cohesive. CSS / SVG mockups show intent — final art should be refined by a designer.

O R U S
Concept 01 · Wordmark

The Spaced Mark

The primary identity: "O R U S" in Manrope ExtraBold, uppercase, with very wide letter-spacing so each letter stands alone in vast, breathing space — "raw nature meets high design." Calm, architectural, and quietly confident. This is the everyday lockup for signage, the menu header and the building façade.

OR/US
Concept 02 · Wordplay Mark

OR / US

The social, flirty variant that splits the name with a molten slash: OR / US. Built for the conversation-starter moments — the stone coasters that read "OR…" on one face and "…US" on the other, date-night collateral, and social campaigns ("Why choose OR when you can choose US?"). A playful secondary lockup that lives alongside the calm primary.

ORUS
Concept 03 · Icon + Wordmark

The Circle of Life

A standalone icon built from the "O" — a perfect circle (生命之圆: wholeness, the eternal rhythm) with a single molten arc of dawn light breaking across its rim, the mouth of the cave at sunrise. It detaches cleanly for app icons, stamps, neon and merch, and encodes courage, energy and "light piercing darkness" in one ownable symbol above the wordmark.

ORUS
Concept 04 · Ink-Seal Icon

The Seal

Expanded from your reference, refined to be deliberate rather than purely organic. The circle stays (the Circle of Life / coaster), but the hand-painted glyph is rebuilt from clean geometric forms: a rounded ore stone with a light-catching aperture (the cave mouth) resting on a solid grounding bar (the horizon — and the bar itself). It still reads as a hanko-style stamp, so it doubles as the physical coaster seal, but with controlled curves and a faint mineral grain instead of loose brushwork. Tonal molten-on-oxide keeps it warm and ownable.

Brand Imagery Guide

Imagery should feel like a secret you descended into — dark, intimate and romantic, with one warm light doing all the work. The mood: a tribe hiding out in the cave, away from the bright corporate surface.

Style — Do

  • Dark, low-key photography with a single warm light piercing the shadow.
  • Grainy 35mm / analog film aesthetic — honest, tactile, never clinical.
  • Macro texture: raw ore, stone coasters, mineral veins, wet glass.
  • Candid, close, intimate human moments — couples, small tribes in the dark.
  • Deep negative space and the widely-spaced wordmark for "high design" calm.

Style — Don't

  • Bright, even, "premium cocktail lounge" or daylight lighting.
  • Staged lifestyle stock photography or fake smiles.
  • Over-saturated neon party clichés or rainbow club lighting.
  • Glossy, plastic, over-polished, digital-feeling surfaces.
  • Clip-art icons or busy layouts that crowd out the breathing room.

Emotion to evoke: the feeling of descending out of the bright, plastic, over-digitised city into something dark, permanent and real — then finding your people there. Mysterious but warm, raw but designed. If an image looks like a glossy ad, it's wrong. If it feels like a single beam of light catching a moment inside a cave, it's right.
