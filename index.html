The Idea Behind the Mark

Every design decision below traces back to the meaning of the name. ORUS isn't a dictionary word — that mystique is the point.

Ore

Raw, unrefined rock from the deepest veins of the earth — holding precious minerals. The palette and textures are mined, not manufactured.

Light in Darkness

The moment light pierces the dark — the mouth of a cave at dawn. This is why a single molten glow always cuts through the near-black.

The Circle "O"

The Circle of Life: wholeness, completeness, the eternal flowing rhythm.

OR / US

Read it again: OR / US. The flirty, social wordplay that resolves the weekend's "this or that" — descend into the cave and just be part of us.

01

Core Values — O.R.U.S.

The name is the manifesto. Read down the letters and you get the four things ORUS exists to do: Open · Relate · Unwind · Stay. Every decision — the dark room, the single beam of light, the stone coasters — serves one of these.

Open

No velvet rope, no posturing, no algorithm deciding who belongs. The door is open to the CBD suit and the weekend wanderer alike, and so is the room — honest, unfiltered, a little raw. "Open" is also a release valve: we're the place you go offline, drop the performance, and finally exhale.

Open door · open people · phones down
Relate

The whole point is the person across the coaster. ORUS is engineered for real connection — the accidental conversation, the flirty "OR… / …US" wingman line, the table that grows by one. In a city talking through screens, we make it easy to relate face to face, unknown to known.

Why choose OR · when you can choose US
Unwind

Everything here is tuned to lower your pulse, not raise it. This is the decompression chamber between the corporate grind and the rest of your night.

Drop the day · lower the pulse
Stay

"Go home early, or stay out late?" Down here the answer is always stay. One more pour, one more song, one more story — the cave is built to keep you a little longer than you planned. Stay close, stay late, stay part of US.

One more pour · stay a while

Flex the acrostic: the same four letters can re-voice the brand for any season or campaign without losing meaning — Our Room, Urban Sanctuary · Ordinary Rituals, Unforgettable Stories · Open, Recharge, Unwind, Socialise · Open Room, United by Spirits.

02

Color Palette

A near-black cave interior, grounded in raw ore and concrete, with one molten beam of dawn light breaking through. The darkness makes the room intimate; the single warm light makes it romantic.

Primary
Cave Black
#0E0E0D
A deep, matte near-black — the dark, intimate interior of the cave. It signals depth, mystery and permanence, pulling guests away from the bright, plastic surface of the city and down into something grounded.
Secondary
Raw Stone
#9C958A
A warm, dusty grey — raw rock and stone coasters. Tactile and honest, it humanises the black and reinforces the "raw nature" half of the brand without feeling cold or corporate.
Accent
Dawn Molten
#E5531A
The light piercing the darkness — the cave mouth at dawn. Warm and magnetic, it triggers appetite, intimacy and anticipation. Used as a single beam, it becomes the brand's most ownable signal.
Accent · Earth
Oxide Vein
#7A3B2E
An oxidised, mineral red-brown — the precious vein running through the ore. It grounds the bright accent, adds maturity and ruggedness, and ties the identity back to earth and hidden treasure.
Neutral
Bone
#EDE7DB
A soft, warm off-white for type and breathing room. Honest rather than clinical, it carries the widely-spaced wordmark cleanly against the dark and keeps the "high design" half polished.

Usage ratio: ~72% Cave Black · ~16% Raw Stone & Bone · ~8% Oxide Vein · ~4% Dawn Molten. The molten light is a single beam — never flood the room with it.

Typography System

The concept deck is explicit: ORUS should read as one bespoke, geometric sans-serif with letters spaced widely apart to evoke vast space and breathing room. Manrope delivers exactly that — a clean, modern geometric sans — so it carries the entire system.

Display · WordmarkManrope — 800 ExtraBold
O R U S
Use for: The logo, hero statements, poster headlines.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE. For the wordmark, very wide tracking (+0.5em) for "vast space." For headlines, +2–4%. Tight leading (1.0). Always add left padding equal to the tracking so it stays optically centered.
HeadingsManrope — 800
Leave the OR behind
Use for: Section titles, campaign headlines.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE, tracking +2–4%. Let one headline dominate a view. Pair the molten accent on a single keyword (e.g. US).
Subheadings · LabelsManrope — 700

Use for: Menu sections, navigation, callouts, coaster text.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE with generous tracking (+0.12em). The spacing keeps the geometric, architectural feel consistent with the wordmark.

Brand Imagery Guide

Imagery should feel like a secret you descended into — dark, intimate and romantic, with one warm light doing all the work. The mood: a tribe hiding out in the cave, away from the bright corporate surface.

Style — Do

  • Grainy 35mm / analog film aesthetic — honest, tactile, never clinical.
  • Macro texture: raw ore, stone coasters, mineral veins, wet glass.
  • Candid, close, intimate human moments — couples, small tribes in the dark.

Style — Don't

  • Staged lifestyle stock photography or fake smiles.
  • Over-saturated neon party clichés or rainbow club lighting.
  • Clip-art icons or busy layouts that crowd out the breathing room.

Emotion to evoke: We set our price low but we do not have to look cheap. The feeling of descending out of the bright, plastic, over-digitised city into something dark, permanent and real — then finding your people there. Mysterious but warm, raw but designed. If an image looks like a glossy ad, it's wrong. If it feels like a single beam of light catching a moment inside a cave, it's right.

Collateral & Touchpoints

Where the brand meets the space. Every physical touchpoint should feel tactile and deliberate — raw surfaces, deep shadow, one warm accent. Cheap on price, never cheap to the touch. Collateral designs are in progress; the direction below sets the tone.

Projected Light & Visuals
After removing the back bars, try adding a single projector throwing light, film grain and texture across the stone — the cave mouth becomes whatever the night calls for. This keeps the space flexible: seasonal artwork, archival film, slow abstract loops, or a fully custom takeover. It is also the more sustainable choice — no reprints, no waste — and lets the venue be re-skinned instantly for private event buyouts.

Coasters & Glass Charms — A Built-in Wingman
In a dark, intimate cave room, ORUS is a prime spot for first dates — so the coaster does the flirting. It comes as a pair: one reads "OR…", the other "…US". A date glances down and the line writes itself — "another one here… OR US heading back?" The name becomes an instant, low-key conversation starter. Extend it with a playful "BEST BEFORE…" series — flirting, last call, dancing, commitment — that regulars want to collect.

Format leans sustainable, not stone: reusable silicone glass charms that live on the stem and go home with regulars, plus recycled-board coasters printed in soy ink. Cheap on price, low on waste, never cheap to the touch.

Menu — Coming Soon

The menu design needs a little more time — it hinges on locking the final logo and the programme first. On format, we like a tactile, low-waste object — a small photo booklet that shows the drinks instead of describing them, layered recycled stock with torn raw edges, die-cut icons, or clean mono type anchored by a single ore-rock mark. On content, here are three directions — each disguises the drink list as something unexpected, while still shouting value and nudging people to order.

Format & Material References
CONCEPT 01
The Payslip

The drink list written in the language of office life — the drinks and categories are named after office jargon (e.g. a "KPI", an "AAR", the "Off-in-Lieu"), framed as a salary statement.

Speaks straight to the after-work crowd escaping the grind, with the punchline "NET — you keep the change."

Drink names / categories Ideas:
KPI · Off-in-Lieu · AAR · Monthly Review · OT Claim
CONCEPT 02
The Market Receipt

The drinks are built on chilled juices, fruit purées, tomato and matcha — real produce — so the menu reads like a fresh-market receipt.

The drinks and categories are named like just-picked goods (a "Fresh Today", the "Cold-Pressed" section), with bold prices shouting value the way a wet-market stall does. Relatable, honest, Singaporean.

Drink names / categories Ideas:
Fresh Today · Cold-Pressed · By the Kilo · Tomato Foam · Best Before Last Call
CONCEPT 03
The Nutrition Panel

Each drink gets its own mock nutrition / ingredients label, riffing on the cold juices, purée highballs, matcha and tomato foam.

The drinks and categories are named after label language (a "100% Fresh Purée", the "% Daily Fun" section), with clean carton typography, servings and a big calorie-style price. Tongue-in-cheek wellness — looks healthy, drinks like a party.

Drink names / categories:
100% Fresh Purée · Matcha · Serving: 1 · Tomato Foam · % Daily Fun

Uniform & Merch

The team and the tribe wear the same mark. Two uniform directions are on the table — references below are high-fashion inspiration, to be re-cut in ORUS colours and branding.

A

Uniform — Two Directions

DIRECTION 01Denim on Denim

Head-to-toe indigo — a jacket-and-trouser or shirt-and-jean pairing in contrasting washes. Hard-wearing, ages with the room, and reads as honest blue-collar craft dressed up.

Leave the cave, leave the planet — and the first colour you see looking back from space is blue: Earth as a single pale blue dot in the dark. Indigo is the most universal, grounding colour we know — the blue of work, of water, of distance, of home.

DIRECTION 02Miner-Style Jumpsuit

A utilitarian one-piece — coverall, boiler suit or bib overall — the literal cave-and-ore, working-the-seam reference. Oversized and rugged: cinched waists, buckle straps, patch and cargo pockets, across charcoal, black, indigo and brown. Worn over a tee, sleeves cut or pushed up. The seal sits on the chest tab and bold across the back.
