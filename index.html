The Idea Behind the Mark

Every design decision below traces back to the meaning of the name. ORUS isn't a dictionary word — that mystique is the point.

Ore

Raw, unrefined rock from the deepest veins of the earth — holding precious minerals. The palette and textures are mined, not manufactured.

Light in Darkness

The moment light pierces the dark — the mouth of a cave at dawn. This is why a single molten glow always cuts through the near-black.

The Circle "O"

The Circle of Life: wholeness, completeness, the eternal flowing rhythm.

OR / US

Read it again: OR / US. The flirty, social wordplay that resolves the weekend's "this or that" — descend into the cave and just be part of us.

01

Core Values — O.R.U.S.

The name is the manifesto. Read down the letters and you get the four things ORUS exists to do: Open · Relate · Unwind · Stay. Every decision — the dark room, the single beam of light, the stone coasters — serves one of these.

Open

No velvet rope, no posturing, no algorithm deciding who belongs. The door is open to the CBD suit and the weekend wanderer alike, and so is the room — honest, unfiltered, a little raw. "Open" is also a release valve: we're the place you go offline, drop the performance, and finally exhale.

Open door · open people · phones down
Relate

The whole point is the person across the coaster. ORUS is engineered for real connection — the accidental conversation, the flirty "OR… / …US" wingman line, the table that grows by one. In a city talking through screens, we make it easy to relate face to face, unknown to known.

Why choose OR · when you can choose US
Unwind

Everything here is tuned to lower your pulse, not raise it. This is the decompression chamber between the corporate grind and the rest of your night.

Drop the day · lower the pulse
Stay

"Go home early, or stay out late?" Down here the answer is always stay. One more pour, one more song, one more story — the cave is built to keep you a little longer than you planned. Stay close, stay late, stay part of US.

One more pour · stay a while

Flex the acrostic: the same four letters can re-voice the brand for any season or campaign without losing meaning — Our Room, Urban Sanctuary · Ordinary Rituals, Unforgettable Stories · Open, Recharge, Unwind, Socialise · Open Room, United by Spirits.

Color Palette

A near-black cave interior, grounded in raw ore and concrete, with one molten beam of dawn light breaking through. The darkness makes the room intimate; the single warm light makes it romantic.

Primary
Cave Black
#0E0E0D
A deep, matte near-black — the dark, intimate interior of the cave. It signals depth, mystery and permanence, pulling guests away from the bright, plastic surface of the city and down into something grounded.
Secondary
Raw Stone
#9C958A
A warm, dusty grey — raw rock and stone coasters. Tactile and honest, it humanises the black and reinforces the "raw nature" half of the brand without feeling cold or corporate.
Accent
Dawn Molten
#E5531A
The light piercing the darkness — the cave mouth at dawn. Warm and magnetic, it triggers appetite, intimacy and anticipation. Used as a single beam, it becomes the brand's most ownable signal.
Accent · Earth
Oxide Vein
#7A3B2E
An oxidised, mineral red-brown — the precious vein running through the ore. It grounds the bright accent, adds maturity and ruggedness, and ties the identity back to earth and hidden treasure.
Neutral
Bone
#EDE7DB
A soft, warm off-white for type and breathing room. Honest rather than clinical, it carries the widely-spaced wordmark cleanly against the dark and keeps the "high design" half polished.

Usage ratio: ~72% Cave Black · ~16% Raw Stone & Bone · ~8% Oxide Vein · ~4% Dawn Molten. The molten light is a single beam — never flood the room with it.

Typography System

The concept deck is explicit: ORUS should read as one bespoke, geometric sans-serif with letters spaced widely apart to evoke vast space and breathing room. Manrope delivers exactly that — a clean, modern geometric sans — so it carries the entire system.

Display · WordmarkManrope — 800 ExtraBold
O R U S
Use for: The logo, hero statements, poster headlines.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE. For the wordmark, very wide tracking (+0.5em) for "vast space." For headlines, +2–4%. Tight leading (1.0). Always add left padding equal to the tracking so it stays optically centered.
HeadingsManrope — 800
Leave the OR behind
Use for: Section titles, campaign headlines.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE, tracking +2–4%. Let one headline dominate a view. Pair the molten accent on a single keyword (e.g. US).
Subheadings · LabelsManrope — 700

Use for: Menu sections, navigation, callouts, coaster text.  ·  Rules: UPPERCASE with generous tracking (+0.12em). The spacing keeps the geometric, architectural feel consistent with the wordmark.

Brand Imagery Guide

Imagery should feel like a secret you descended into — dark, intimate and romantic, with one warm light doing all the work. The mood: a tribe hiding out in the cave, away from the bright corporate surface.

Style — Do

  • Dark, low-key photography with a single warm light piercing the shadow.
  • Grainy 35mm / analog film aesthetic — honest, tactile, never clinical.
  • Macro texture: raw ore, stone coasters, mineral veins, wet glass.
  • Candid, close, intimate human moments — couples, small tribes in the dark.
  • Deep negative space and the widely-spaced wordmark for "high design" calm.

Style — Don't

  • Bright, even, "premium cocktail lounge" or daylight lighting.
  • Staged lifestyle stock photography or fake smiles.
  • Over-saturated neon party clichés or rainbow club lighting.
  • Glossy, plastic, over-polished, digital-feeling surfaces.
  • Clip-art icons or busy layouts that crowd out the breathing room.
