The Vision

A young, bold and relatable RTD line in a flat glass bottle — one you grab because it looks sharp, and keep because you actually know what's inside.

We're locking the format as a flat, flask-style glass bottle — not a can. Clear (or tinted) glass that shows the liquid, a short neck, a metal screw cap, and a label that owns the flat face. Premium enough for a fridge door, portable enough for a party table. The inspirations below are all built on that form factor — and show how ORUS can do it our own way.

YoungPlayful, unserious energy. Bold type on a flat face, saturated liquid colour showing through glass. It should feel made by people our age, not a legacy distillery.
BoldOne idea per bottle, at maximum scale. The flat face is a billboard — the ORUS Seal, a mood name, or a colour field — instant recognition on a shelf or a bar top.
RelatableCurious names spark the pick-up; plain-language flavour + ABV close the deal. No pretension, no guessing. Honest value, just like the bar.

Signature Design — Flat Glass Stickers

The can RTD label system — hazy Status Colour waves + exploded Seal "O" + mood name + clarity line — moves onto glass as a full-face matte sticker on the flat flask. Same artwork, same hierarchy; the surface changes from aluminium to glass. Volume line updates to glass format (e.g. 200ML).

  • Carry-over from cansThe locked label system stays: ORUS top · giant Seal O · mood name · flavour + ABV. Only the substrate changes — flat glass instead of a can wrap.
  • Sticker formatFull-face matte panel on the flat front (with optional wrap to the sides). Spot-gloss on the Seal O for the tactile hit.
  • Status ColoursMolten Hot & Bothered · Verdigris Open Tab · Sulphur Slow Hands · Ore Black Left on Read — same four-SKU family.
  • The NameCheeky mood names stay: Hot & Bothered, Open Tab, Slow Hands, Left on Read.
  • Clarity LinePlain flavour + ABV + volume under the name — e.g. YUZU & UME SHOCHU SPRITZ · 5.5% · 200ML.
  • FinishMatte body sticker, spot-gloss Seal — glass reads premium; colour still shouts.

Seal on Glass — Toolkit

The Seal is still the flexible asset — now designed for a flat glass face, not a curved can. Same motif logic; different surface. Three production-friendly cues on the same flat flask form — embossed glass skipped as a cost tier we don’t need.

01 · Seal as Face Graphic
Crop the Seal large across the flat label so it reads as a giant "O" — portal / cave mouth / open door. Hero for the core line.
Flat flask · face owns the panel
02 · Seal as Window
Die-cut or transparent oval in the label so the liquid colour (or macro fruit) shows through the Seal's aperture. Flavour you can see.
Flat flask · aperture = product reveal
03 · Seal as Cap Stamp
Small Seal on the cap sticker / neck seal — quiet craft cue that ties every SKU back to ORUS even when the body label flexes.
Flat flask · seal on the cap

More levers — Status Colour as full-bleed wrap; Seal as a small circular stamp over bold type (like the "for the love of the mix" marks in the refs); neck collar with ABV; back-face QR / playlist. One flat bottle, endless system.

Positioning & Scenarios

产品定位与消费场景 (Product Positioning & Scenarios)
The RTD line extends ORUS's "Third Space" philosophy beyond the physical bar — a flat glass bottle that feels like a keepable object, not a disposable can.

Single Drinking (单人饮用)
For winding down at home or creative work sessions. Low-ABV or tea-based options (like Left On Read) that offer complex flavours without the heavy hit — glass that looks good on a desk.
Party & Social (聚会场景)
High-energy, conversation-starting bottles to bring to a dinner or house gathering. The bold names act as icebreakers; the flat flask passes around cleanly.
Retail Display (零售陈列)
Designed for shelf impact. Lined up, the flat faces and Status Colours create a striking billboard block — stronger silhouette than round glass or cans.
